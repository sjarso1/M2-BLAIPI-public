Project Overview

M2-BLAIPI is a biomedical-engineering research project developing a biologically enriched framework for Burkitt lymphoma risk modeling. The project asks whether metabolic pathway activity inferred from transcriptomic data, combined with molecular subgroup information, can complement the clinical variables used in current prognostic tools.

Why This Work Matters

Burkitt lymphoma is an aggressive B-cell malignancy and a leading cause of childhood cancer mortality in malaria-endemic regions of sub-Saharan Africa, where survival outcomes remain substantially worse than in high-income settings. The Burkitt Lymphoma International Prognostic Index (BL-IPI) relies primarily on clinical variables and does not capture the biological heterogeneity revealed by recent genomic studies. M2-BLAIPI investigates whether metabolic pathway activity and molecular subgroup information can add prognostic value beyond established clinical risk factors.

Research Aims

  1. Identify metabolic pathway signatures associated with genetic subgroups.
  2. Develop biologically motivated metabolic features and composite scores.
  3. Evaluate a multivariate risk-modeling framework.

Current Workstreams

  • Biology and literature synthesis
  • Pathway activity analysis
  • Feature engineering
  • Model development and validation planning
